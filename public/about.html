Growing Up...

My name is Anton Sebastian Bilbaeno.

I was born in San Jose, California and moved to Beaverton, Oregon at the age of four, in 1994. I have lived in Oregon ever since.

When we came to Oregon we moved in with my grandmother, in the same house where my mother herself grew up. I miss being in a home with more than one generation. Shortly after, we moved to an apartment near the intersection of Highway 26 and 217. We lived in that apartment for a little while and then moved a few blocks away to a house that we rented. We lived in that house for a little while and then moved back towards the apartment to a house that my mother bought. My mother lived there until she retired and sold it.

Beaverton is where I grew up, though I would not choose to live to live in the suburbs now. You have to drive everywhere, but there isn't anywhere to go. The city is a little bit better. I can bike or ride the bus almost anywhere. Otherwise, I'd live in a more rural area or outside the US.

My mother was very frugal. I do not know how, but she saved enough for the downpayment on that house and for both my brother and I to go to college without governent loans. All of those on sale items at the grocery store do add up...

Stability aside, my mother showed me discipline and what it means to commit to something. Growing up, I starting playing soccer at a young age. I went on to play competitive soccer, sometimes known as "club" soccer in the US. I tried out for and was accepted to a very good team. A few years later, I didn't make the cut. I tried out for another team and they took me on. After being accepted to the new team, the old one called me back. Of course I wanted to go back, but I had already made a commitment, and my mother told me that I must honor it. I'm sure I resented her at the time, but I wouldn't change that decision.

My mother is one of the biggest factors in me being "successful". I will limit my definition of "success" strictly to the fact that: I am privileged to be able to see hope into the future for myself and have enough time and energy to learn and apply myself to nearly anything.

I was named after a Russian author by my father. Anton Chekhov has a talent of being able to distill everyday interactions of human life into captivating stories that almost teach a lesson themselves. The stories, of course, never explicity "spell out" their true meaning and usually end abruptly, but the abrupt endings give you an opportunity to reflect immediately. He is sometimes known as of the greatest writers of short fiction stories. Some of Chekhov's writings may seem mundane or inconclusive, but they are for you to make your own interpretation, as is all art.
