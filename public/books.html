  • The Philosphy of Teaching by Arnold Tompkins (1893)

    It is interesting that this book was published so long ago, yet schools around the world (I only know the US) remain in the state of producing workers for factories. This book discusses the processes by which a mind comes to know something and the teacher's duty of providing the student with the means to reach a point of knowledge on their own -- in contrast to simply regurgitating knowledge. The book also discusses what it means to be a teacher -- a position of the highest moral obligation.
